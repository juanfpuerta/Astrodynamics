\section{Executive Course Audit \& Rationale}
The predecessor course layout (Semester 2026-1) established a robust baseline in absolute tracking and perturbed physical modeling. However, to meet the modern demands of 4th-year aerospace engineering students, the 2026-2 syllabus deepens computational and mathematical rigor across four critical areas:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Transition to Constrained Trajectory Optimization:} Rather than utilizing simple root-finding differential targeting ($f(x) = 0$), students will formulate continuous, multi-variable steering histories ($\mathbf{u}(t)$) using Pontryagin's Minimum Principle and state-to-costate mappings.
    \item \textbf{Mathematical Rigor in High-Fidelity Deep Space Targets:} Interplanetary targeting moves from sphere-of-influence matching to explicit multi-variable 3D \textbf{B-Plane Navigation} ($\mathbf{B}\cdot\mathbf{T}$, $\mathbf{B}\cdot\mathbf{R}$).
    \item \textbf{Conservation Auditing in Multi-Body Regimes:} Students will evaluate the preservation of the Jacobi Constant ($C$) within the synodic Circular Restricted Three-Body Problem (CR3BP) and identify why standard symplectic integration methods degrade under rotating Coriolis forces.
    \item \textbf{Stochastic Estimation \& Proximity Operations:} Orbit Determination is updated to contrast Extended Kalman Filters (EKF) with Unscented Kalman Filters (UKF). This is paired with close-proximity relative maneuvering via the Hill-Clohessy-Wiltshire (HCW) formulation.
\end{enumerate}

\section{General Course Objectives}
Upon successful completion of this advanced module, students will be able to:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Formulate Coupled Non-Spherical Loops:} Code vectorized perturbations mapping zonal harmonics alongside dynamic drag profiles to analyze orbital decay.
    \item \textbf{Design 3D Interplanetary B-Plane Targets:} Map heliocentric Lambert arcs into planetocentric hyperbolic asymptotes to steer gravity assists via targeter vectors.
    \item \textbf{Program Non-Keplerian Symplectic Sandboxes:} Implement specialized implicit solvers to model halo orbits and cislunar manifold pipelines.
    \item \textbf{Expose Nonlinear Filter Divergence:} Demonstrate why Jacobian linearizations diverge under high uncertainties and execute derivative-free UKF filters.
\end{itemize}

\section{Detailed Week-by-Week Course Structure}

\subsection*{Module 1: High-Fidelity Orbital Perturbations \& Coupled Loops}
\begin{description}
    \item[Week 1: Foundations of Non-Keplerian Acceleration] \\
    Vector formulation of non-conservative and third-body perturbation accelerations $\mathbf{a}_d$. Derivation of Lagrange's Planetary Equations (LPE) and Gauss's Variational Equations (GVE) mapping element rate changes:
    \begin{equation}
        \frac{da}{dt} = \frac{2 a^2}{h} \left( e \sin \nu \cdot R + \frac{p}{r} \cdot S \right)
    \end{equation}
    
    \item[Week 2: Zonal Harmonics \& The Geopotential Spectrum] \\
    Expansion of the Earth's non-spherical potential using Legendre polynomials. Derivation of the secular drift rates for the ascending node ($\dot{\Omega}$) and argument of perigee ($\dot{\omega}$) caused by Earth's oblateness ($J_2$).
    
    \item[Week 3: Advanced Constrained Orbit Design] \\
    Sun-Synchronous Orbit (SSO) design constraints matching equatorial nodal precession rates to the Earth's mean heliocentric motion. Derivation of frozen-orbit conditions ($\dot{\omega} = 0$) at critical inclinations ($i = 63.435^\circ$ or $116.565^\circ$).
    
    \item[Week 4: Non-Conservative Force Coupling \& Lifetime Loops] \\
    Atmospheric drag integration featuring dynamic ballistic coefficients ($B = C_D A / m$) and exponential density profiles. Coupling Solar Radiation Pressure (SRP) forces with shadows.
    \textbf{Computational Lab 1:} Develop a vectorized $J_2$-drag propagator in Python to compute orbital decay lifetimes.
\end{description}

\subsection*{Module 2: 3D Interplanetary Trajectories \& B-Plane Navigation}
\begin{description}
    \item[Week 5: 3D Patched Conics \& Hyperbolic Asymptotes] \\
    Expanding 2D heliocentric Lambert transfers into 3D inertial space. Analytical calculation of the hyperbolic departure right ascension ($\beta$) and declination ($\delta$). Mapping of launch geometry boundaries to avoid the "inclination trap" ($i \ge |\delta|$).
    
    \item[Week 6: Gravity Assist Mechanics \& Powered Flybys] \\
    Momentum transfer physics within a planetary Sphere of Influence (SOI). Derivation of the turning angle ($\delta$) as a function of periapsis radius ($r_p$):
    \begin{equation}
        \sin\left(\frac{\delta}{2}\right) = \frac{1}{1 + \frac{r_p v_{\infty}^2}{\mu}}
    \end{equation}
    Formulating trailing vs. leading flybys to optimize heliocentric energy gains or losses.
    
    \item[Week 7: The B-Plane Targeting Framework] \\
    Defining the B-Plane coordinate axes ($\mathbf{S}, \mathbf{T}, \mathbf{R}$) perpendicular to the incoming velocity vector $\mathbf{v}_\infty$. Projecting the miss vector $\mathbf{B}$ to obtain target parameters $\mathbf{B}\cdot\mathbf{T}$ and $\mathbf{B}\cdot\mathbf{R}$. Implementing multi-variable target finders in NASA's GMAT.
    \textbf{Midterm Examination:} A 24-hour take-home analytical and numerical multi-stage trajectory design challenge.
\end{description}

\subsection*{Module 3: Continuous Thrust \& Non-Linear Trajectory Optimization}
\begin{description}
    \item[Week 8: Low-Thrust Flight Mechanics \& Expanding States] \\
    Low-thrust Solar Electric Propulsion (SEP) modeling. Expanding state-space vectors to 7D systems by coupling continuous propellant mass depletion ($\dot{m} = -\frac{T}{I_{sp} g_0}$). Analytical treatments utilizing Edelbaum's asymptotic steering equations.
    
    \item[Week 9: Numerical Integration of Low-Thrust Regimes] \\
    Constructing custom 7D derivative files to simulate tangential, radial, and normal steering schedules. Investigating continuous gravity losses ($\Delta V_{spiral} > \Delta V_{Hohmann}$) during long-duration spiral maneuvers.
    \textbf{Computational Lab 2:} Code and run a custom 7D low-thrust RK-4 solver, cross-validating the output against GMAT's continuous finite burn model.
    
    \item[Week 10: Trajectory Optimization \& Optimal Control Theory] \\
    Contrasting Root-Finding Targeters with Non-Linear Programming (NLP) Optimizers. Formulating the pseudo-Hamiltonian $\mathcal{H}$ and costate variables ($\boldsymbol{\lambda}$) under Pontryagin's Minimum Principle:
    \begin{equation}
        \mathcal{H} = \mathbf{f}(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{u}, t)^T \boldsymbol{\lambda} + L(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{u}, t)
    \end{equation}
    Exploring single shooting, multiple shooting, and collocation schemes to solve boundary-value problems.
\end{description}

\subsection*{Module 4: Multi-Body Dynamics \& Symplectic Numerical Sandbox}
\begin{description}
    \item[Week 11: The Synodic Frame \& CR3BP Equations of Motion] \\
    Derivation of the equations of motion in the rotating (synodic) frame centered at the primary-secondary barycenter. Isolating the Jacobi Constant ($C$) as the system's fundamental conservation metric:
    \begin{equation}
        C = 2\Omega(x, y, z) - (v_x^2 + v_y^2 + v_z^2)
    \end{equation}
    Mapping Zero-Velocity Surfaces to define physical boundary limits.
    
    \item[Week 12: Libration Points, Halo Orbits, \& Numerical Breakdown] \\
    Linearizing dynamics around the collinear equilibrium points ($L_1, L_2, L_3$). Analytical generation of periodic Halo and quasi-periodic Lissajous initial conditions using Richardson's third-order expansions.
    \textbf{Computational Lab 3:} Write an implicit Gauss-collocation or velocity-extended splitting integrator to demonstrate why standard non-symplectic integrators artificially destroy the system energy over 100 periods.
    
    \item[Week 13: Invariant Manifold Theory \& Cislunar Transit] \\
    Eigenstructure tracking of the Monodromy Matrix to identify stable and unstable invariant manifolds. Generating orbit-insertion "tubes" that act as free paths to and from libration orbits. Application to low-energy lunar captures (e.g., GRAIL mission profile).
\end{description}

\subsection*{Module 5: Stochastic Orbit Estimation \& Distributed Space Systems}
\begin{description}
    \item[Week 14: Initial Orbit Determination \& Sensor Realities] \\
    Geometric foundations of Initial Orbit Determination (IOD). Solving the three-point position vector boundary problem via Gibbs' method. Formulating Gauss's angles-only optical tracking solver using iterative $f$ and $g$ series expansions.
    
    \item[Week 15: Extended vs. Unscented Kalman Filtering] \\
    Recursive stochastic state estimation algorithms. Demonstrating EKF linearization divergence when propagating covariance ($\mathbf{P}_{k|k}$) through highly non-linear regimes. The Unscented Transform: Generating Sigma Points to capture true mean and covariance mappings up to 3rd-order Taylor expansions:
    \begin{equation}
        \boldsymbol{\chi}_i = \hat{\mathbf{x}} \pm \left( \sqrt{(n + \lambda)\mathbf{P}} \right)_i
    \end{equation}
    Transforming coordinates from ECI to RIC frames for Conjunction Risk assessments.
    
    \item[Week 16: Relative Orbit Dynamics \& Formation Flying] \\
    Linearizing relative motion in the Local-Vertical Local-Horizontal (LVLH) frame. Integrating the classical Hill-Clohessy-Wiltshire (HCW) equations:
    \begin{align}
        \ddot{x} - 2n\dot{y} - 3n^2x &= f_x / m \\
        \ddot{y} + 2n\dot{x} &= f_y / m \\
        \ddot{z} + n^2z &= f_z / m
    \end{align}
    Designing closed relative passive ellipses for virtual formation instruments.
\end{description}

\section{Pedagogical Rules \& AI-Proofing Framework}
To enforce academic honesty and foster authentic problem-solving skills, this course implements a "Trust but Verify" methodology with structural computational checks:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Forbidden Black-Box Modules in Labs:} For specific computational milestones, the use of GMAT's native differential corrector target loops or Python's \texttt{scipy.optimize} functions is strictly forbidden. Students must write their own Newton-Raphson solvers from scratch, proving they understand the Jacobians and State Transition Matrices ($\boldsymbol{\Phi}(t, t_0)$) before using professional commercial suites.
    \item \textbf{Forensic Bug Audits:} Instead of standard "design-from-scratch" tasks, students will be handed working scripts containing subtle, intentional physics flaws (e.g., continuous low-thrust out-of-plane steering without node-chopping, or violating the $i < |\delta|$ constraint). Grading is based on the student's ability to locate the bug, mathematically demonstrate the efficiency penalty, and implement the correct fix.
    \item \textbf{Mandatory Cross-Frame Verification:} Every mathematical log must have a corresponding, scale-accurate 3D visualization. If a student's text report describes a warped three-body transfer but their integrated code plots a standard circular orbit due to reference frame coordinate mismatches, the visual variance will highlight the issue during grading.
\end{enumerate}
