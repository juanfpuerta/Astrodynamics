\section*{1. Problem Statement}
Using a Lambert Problem solver, the optimal launch window for a 2026 Earth-Mars transfer has been identified. The Python physics engine outputted the following Geocentric Hyperbolic Departure Asymptote vector ($\boldsymbol{v}_\infty$) for the epoch \textbf{01 Nov 2026 00:00:00}:
\begin{equation}
    \boldsymbol{v}_\infty = [-1.7788,\ 2.4358,\ 0.2965] \text{ km/s}
\end{equation}
The spacecraft is currently in a 300 km circular Low Earth Orbit (LEO) with an inclination of $28.5^\circ$. \textbf{Objective:} Analytically calculate the Trans-Mars Injection (TMI) maneuver ($\Delta V$), map the departure angles ($\beta$ and $\delta$) to the parking orbit's Right Ascension of the Ascending Node ($\Omega$), and configure the GMAT numerical targeter.

\section*{2. Analytical Solution}

\textbf{Known Earth Constants:} $\mu_\oplus = 398600.4415 \text{ km}^3/\text{s}^2$, $R_\oplus = 6378.14 \text{ km}$. \\
\textbf{Parking Orbit Radius:} $r_{park} = 6378.14 + 300 = 6678.14 \text{ km}$.

\subsection*{Step 2.1: Departure Energy ($C_3$) and $\Delta V_{TMI}$}
First, we find the magnitude of the hyperbolic excess velocity, $v_\infty$:
\begin{equation}
    v_\infty = \sqrt{(-1.7788)^2 + (2.4358)^2 + (0.2965)^2} = \sqrt{9.185} \approx \textbf{3.03 km/s}
\end{equation}
The Characteristic Energy ($C_3$) is $v_\infty^2 = \textbf{9.18 km}^2/\text{s}^2$. We now calculate the velocity of the circular parking orbit ($V_{circ}$) and the required velocity at the periapsis of the escape hyperbola ($V_p$):
\begin{align*}
    V_{circ} &= \sqrt{\frac{\mu_\oplus}{r_{park}}} = \sqrt{\frac{398600.4415}{6678.14}} = 7.725 \text{ km/s} \\
    V_p &= \sqrt{C_3 + \frac{2\mu_\oplus}{r_{park}}} = \sqrt{9.18 + 119.37} \approx 11.338 \text{ km/s}
\end{equation}
The required impulsive TMI maneuver magnitude is:
\begin{equation}
    \Delta V_{TMI} = V_p - V_{circ} = 11.338 - 7.725 = \textbf{3.61 km/s}
\end{equation}

\subsection*{Step 2.2: The Asymptote Angles ($\beta$ and $\delta$)}
We convert the 3D Cartesian vector into spherical coordinates to find the Right Ascension ($\beta$) and Declination ($\delta$):
\begin{align*}
    \delta &= \arcsin\left(\frac{v_z}{v_\infty}\right) = \arcsin\left(\frac{0.2965}{3.03}\right) = \textbf{5.61}^\circ \\
    \beta &= \text{arctan2}(v_y, v_x) = \text{arctan2}(2.4358, -1.7788) = \textbf{126.14}^\circ
\end{align*}
\textit{Check Geometric Validity:} Our parking orbit inclination ($i = 28.5^\circ$) is strictly greater than $|\delta|$ ($5.61^\circ$). The maneuver is geometrically possible without an expensive plane change.

\subsection*{Step 2.3: Aligning the Parking Orbit ($\Omega$)}
To align the orbital plane with the departure asymptote, the Right Ascension of the Ascending Node ($\Omega$) must satisfy:
\begin{equation}
    \sin(\Omega - \beta) = \frac{-\tan(\delta)}{\tan(i)}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation*}
    \sin(\Omega - 126.14^\circ) = \frac{-\tan(5.61^\circ)}{\tan(28.5^\circ)} = \frac{-0.0982}{0.5429} = -0.1808
\end{equation*}
Taking the arcsine gives an offset of $-10.42^\circ$. Solving for the primary $\Omega$:
\begin{equation}
    \Omega_1 = 126.14^\circ - 10.42^\circ = \textbf{115.72}^\circ
\end{equation}

\section*{3. GMAT Architecture Configuration}
With the analytical physics solved, we bridge the data into the numerical environment. 

\subsection*{3.1 Spacecraft Initialization}
Input the analytically derived RAAN to perfectly align the parking orbit in 3D space:
\begin{lstlisting}
MarsSC.Epoch = '01 Nov 2026 00:00:00.000';
MarsSC.SMA = 6678.0;   % 300 km altitude parking orbit
MarsSC.ECC = 0.0;      % Circular orbit
MarsSC.INC = 28.5;     % Validated: i > |Delta|
MarsSC.RAAN = 115.72;  % Mathematically aligned to Beta and Delta
MarsSC.AOP = 0.0;
MarsSC.TA = 0.0;       % Initialized at 0; Targeter will find the burn point
\end{lstlisting}

\subsection*{3.2 The Coast-to-Burn Targeter}
We use the analytical $\Delta V_{TMI}$ ($3.61 \text{ km/s}$) as our initial guess for the burn. We implement a \texttt{CoastTime} variable to allow GMAT to naturally propagate the spacecraft to the exact required True Anomaly (burn location) before firing the engines.

\begin{lstlisting}
Create Variable CoastTime;
CoastTime = 0.04; % Initial guess: roughly 1 hour in days

BeginMissionSequence;

Target DefaultDC {SolveMode = Solve, ExitMode = DiscardAndContinue};
    
    % 1. Spin the dial to find the perfect burn location (Beta alignment)
    Vary DefaultDC(CoastTime = 0.04, {Lower = 0.0, Upper = 0.1, MaxStep = 0.02});
    
    % 2. Adjust the TMI Burn Magnitude to achieve the exact C3
    Vary DefaultDC(ImpulsiveBurn1.Element1 = 3.61, {MaxStep = 0.2});
    
    % 3. Coast and Burn
    Propagate EarthProp(MarsSC) {MarsSC.ElapsedDays = CoastTime};
    Maneuver ImpulsiveBurn1(MarsSC);
    
    % 4. Fly to Mars & Target B-Plane
    Propagate EarthProp(MarsSC) {MarsSC.Earth.RMAG = 900000};
    Propagate SunProp(MarsSC) {MarsSC.Mars.Periapsis};
    
    Achieve DefaultDC(MarsSC.Mars_Body.BdotT = -6000, {Tolerance = 0.1});
    Achieve DefaultDC(MarsSC.Mars_Body.BdotR = 6000, {Tolerance = 0.1});

EndTarget;
\end{lstlisting}
