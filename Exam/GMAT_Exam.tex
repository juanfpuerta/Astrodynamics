\section*{Problem 1: Low-Thrust Escape Spiral (50 Points)}
Configure an \texttt{ElectricThruster} in GMAT to simulate a spiral escape from Earth to $C_3 = 0$. 
\begin{enumerate}
    \item[(a)] Detail your targeting sequence variables. Why did you choose your specific \texttt{Perturbation} step size?
    \item[(b)] Provide a plot of the spacecraft's Semi-Major Axis ($a$) vs. Elapsed Days.
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Problem 2: B-Plane Targeting (50 Points)}
You are capturing a probe into Martian orbit.
\begin{enumerate}
    \item[(a)] Using the \texttt{Target} sequence, vary the incoming \texttt{BdotT} and \texttt{BdotR} parameters to achieve a polar insertion orbit.
    \item[(b)] Document the $\Delta V$ cost. If you included solar radiation pressure (SRP) in your force model, explain how it shifted your targeted insertion point compared to a pure point-mass model.
\end{enumerate}
