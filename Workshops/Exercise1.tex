\section*{Scenario: The Martian Intercept}
We are launching a 1,500 kg scientific probe to Mars. The spacecraft is currently parked in a 300 km circular Low Earth Orbit (LEO). You must calculate the departure energy, target a specific arrival trajectory using the B-Plane, and analyze the sensitivity of the launch window.

\section*{Part 1: The Analytical Baseline (Colab) -- [15 Minutes]}
Open your Python/Colab patched conic scripts. Assuming a Hohmann transfer from Earth to Mars (assuming circular, coplanar planetary orbits for this baseline approximation):
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Calculate the required departure hyperbolic excess velocity ($\boldsymbol{v}_\infty$) and the specific energy ($C_3$).
    \item Calculate the impulsive $\Delta V$ required to inject the spacecraft from the 300 km LEO parking orbit onto the interplanetary escape hyperbola.
\end{enumerate}
\textit{Record these values. They are your initial guesses for the GMAT numerical solver.}

\section*{Part 2: B-Plane Targeting (GMAT) -- [30 Minutes]}
You have been provided with a baseline GMAT script (\texttt{Mars\_BPlane\_Base.script}) containing the spacecraft, a heliocentric propagator, and the Earth-Mars celestial mechanics.

Your task is to configure the \texttt{DifferentialCorrector} to achieve a polar Martian flyby at an altitude of 500 km.
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Set up a \texttt{Vary} command for the Trans-Mars Injection (TMI) burn. Vary the Velocity (\texttt{Element1}), Normal (\texttt{Element2}), and Binormal (\texttt{Element3}) components.
    \item Use the B-Plane to target the arrival. Set up your \texttt{Achieve} commands:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \texttt{Achieve DC(Sat.Mars.BdotT = 0.0, \{Tolerance = 0.1\});} (Forces the polar geometry)
        \item \texttt{Achieve DC(Sat.Mars.RMAG = 3896.2, \{Tolerance = 0.1\});} (500 km altitude + Mars radius)
    \end{itemize}
    \item Run the sequence. Record the final numerical $\Delta V$ components. How do they compare to your Colab approximation?
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Part 3: The Launch Window (GMAT) -- [15 Minutes]}
Planetary alignments wait for no one. 
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Go to the spacecraft's initial state and shift the \texttt{Epoch} forward by exactly \textbf{48 hours} (representing a launch delay).
    \item Re-run your Differential Corrector target sequence.
    \item Record the new $\Delta V$ required to hit the exact same B-Plane target. 
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Deliverable: Chief Architect's Memo (CAM)}
Submit a single-page PDF containing your Colab baseline outputs, the GMAT numerical outputs, and a concluding CAM paragraph. 

\textbf{CAM Prompt:} \textit{Based on your findings in Part 3, mathematically explain why the $\Delta V$ penalty occurred when the launch was delayed by 48 hours. Connect your explanation back to the patched conic planetary phasing you modeled in Part 1.}
